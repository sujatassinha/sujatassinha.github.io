About

Research scientist building
principled AI infrastructure

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Engineering at Virginia Tech and a visiting researcher at Argonne National Laboratory (DOE). My work sits at the intersection of information theory, statistical modeling, and large-scale computing systems — with a focus on deriving the fundamental mathematical limits that determine how efficiently data can be compressed, stored, and learned from.

At Argonne, I delivered the first tile-aware, finite-blocklength rate-distortion bounds for HPC-grade lossy compressors — enabling compressor engineers to know, for the first time, how close their codec is to the theoretical ceiling and where algorithmic headroom remains. This directly informs parameter selection for production-grade compressors like SZ3 and SPERR running on DOE leadership-class systems.

My current research extends these foundations to LLM pretraining: using token-based rate-distortion theory to mathematically bound the scaling law surface measured empirically by Kaplan et al. and Chinchilla — predicting the compute-optimal (N*, D*) split before a single training run begins, with implications for GPU budget allocation at frontier AI labs.

Prior to academia, I co-founded Pulse Prognostics, an early experience that shaped my conviction: the most durable competitive advantage in any technical domain comes from understanding the underlying limits, not just optimizing within them.

Research

Three problem areas,
one theoretical thread

Each project extends classical information theory to a regime where it previously failed — and delivers engineering results that practitioners can directly apply.

01 / 03
Data Compression · HPC

Tile-Aware Rate-Distortion Theory for Scientific Data

Classical RD theory predicts bitrates far higher than what production compressors achieve on HPC data — because it assumes global stationarity and infinite blocklengths that parallel compressors never have. I developed the first piecewise 2D Gaussian random field model with tile-aware finite-blocklength bounds, validated against SZ3 and SPERR on four DOE simulation datasets.

Industry value
Replaces empirical tile-size sweeps with theory-guided parameter selection. Directly applicable to codec design pipelines at HPC centers.
Piecewise 2D GRFFinite BlocklengthSPERR · SZ3SDRBench
02 / 03
AI Research · Quantitative

Information-Theoretic Foundations of LLM Scaling Laws

The power-law scaling curves measured by Kaplan et al. and Chinchilla are empirical — no one has proved why they hold. I am building the first derivation: treating pretraining as directed information compression over tokens (Bai 2025 semantic RD framework) and bounding the loss surface as a function of model size N, dataset size D, and compute budget C. The result predicts optimal (N*, D*) before training begins.

Industry value
Reduces multi-million-dollar training allocation risk. Applicable to any organization running large-scale pretraining or fine-tuning.

03 / 03
AI Research · Security

Adversarial Robustness Under Unknown Channel Transformations

When adversarial perturbations pass through an unknown wireless channel, standard FGSM and PGM attacks become unpredictable and brittle. CDI-aware GANs learn the distribution over transformed perturbations, producing a single defender model that is robust to multiple attack strategies simultaneously — demonstrated on 5G modulation classification with practical SNR ranges.

Industry value
Applicable to any ML classifier deployed in adversarial physical environments: RF systems, sensor networks, autonomous systems.

Vision

Building theory that outlasts
the current generation of tools

"The most durable competitive advantage in any technical domain comes from understanding the fundamental limits — not just optimizing within them."

Whether it is the 20 petabytes from a single NYX cosmology run, the I/O bottleneck of a combustion simulation checkpoint, or the trillion tokens consumed during LLM pretraining — the same question underlies all of it: what are the fundamental limits, and how far are we from them?

My work answers this with closed-form mathematics, validates it against real systems at DOE scale, and translates it into design guidance that engineers can use today — without waiting for theory to become textbook knowledge.

The ambition is a coherent theory of resource-constrained intelligence: principled, hardware-independent, and applicable across data compression, AI training, and HPC system design.

Rigor first
Every bound is proved, every claim validated against real data and real compressors. Theory that cannot be tested is not useful theory.
Systems scale
From Argonne's Bebop cluster to trillion-parameter models — the work must inform decisions engineers actually face in production environments.
Open & reproducible
All experimental code, datasets, and mathematical derivations released publicly. Science that cannot be reproduced is not science.

I am a fifth-year PhD candidate at MIT CSAIL advised by Prof. Jane Smith, working at the intersection of large language models, reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF), and efficient inference. My research aims to make powerful AI systems more reliable, interpretable, and deployable at scale.

Prior to MIT, I completed my B.S. in Computer Science at Stanford University with honors. I have spent three summers as a research intern at Google DeepMind, OpenAI, and Meta AI Research, where my work on model compression shipped in production systems serving millions of users.

I thrive in environments that blend principled inquiry with engineering rigor. I am actively seeking research scientist and applied ML roles starting Summer / Fall 2025 where I can continue pushing the frontier of language model capabilities and alignment.

Research

Areas of focus

01
Language Model Alignment

Developing scalable RLHF and constitutional AI methods that align model behavior with human intent without sacrificing capability or introducing deceptive tendencies.

02
Efficient Inference

Designing quantization, distillation, and speculative decoding pipelines that reduce inference cost by 4–8× while preserving benchmark performance on complex reasoning tasks.

03
Interpretability & Probing

Mechanistically understanding emergent capabilities in transformer architectures — identifying circuits responsible for in-context learning, factual recall, and multi-step reasoning.
